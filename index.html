I completed my undergraduate studies in Chemistry and Mathematics at Northeastern University in 2018, and I recently defended my Ph.D. on 14 March 2023 at the University of Michigan in Chemistry and Scientific Computing. My dissertation focused on computational and theoretical advances in quantum chemical methods. My general interests include:

  • high-performance computing
  • computational chemistry and physics
  • electronic structure theory
  • data analysis
  • machine learning
  • programming languages
My graduate funding was provided by the National Science Foundation through the Graduate Research Fellowship program (GRFP). I also received funding from the Department of Energy and the University of Michigan Chemistry Department and Rackham Graduate School.

I have recently started experimenting more with CUDA/C++, the Rust programming language, and various numerical libraries in Python. I will try documenting my experience on this site more regularly as time allows.
